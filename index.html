Comprehensive Guide to GST Structure & Process Archetypes

Process Archetypes

These four archetypal patterns capture the most common GST value chain structures across different industries:

🌾 Raw Agricultural to Processed Food

Starts with an exempt farm product, involves primary processing (mandi, miller), and ends with a packaged, branded retail product. Often features RCM on intermediary services and an initial break in the ITC chain.

Applicable HSN Chapters:
04 (Dairy), 08 (Fruits & Nuts), 10 (Cereals), 12 (Oil Seeds)

⛏️ Raw Mineral to Finished Industrial Good

Begins with mining/extraction, moves through heavy industrial processing (smelting, refining), and results in finished goods for construction or further manufacturing. Features a continuous ITC chain and non-GST levies like royalties.

Applicable HSN Chapters:
25 (Stone, Cement), 26 (Ores), 72 (Iron & Steel), 76 (Aluminium)

🔧 Multi-Stage Component Assembly

Involves sourcing numerous high-tax components, followed by factory assembly and testing. The final product is often subject to both a high GST rate and a Compensation Cess.

Applicable HSN Chapters:
84 (Machinery, engines), 85 (Electrical Machinery), 87 (Vehicles)

🧵 Fibre to Finished Apparel (Inverted Duty)

A chain from raw fibre through spinning, weaving, chemical processing (dyeing), and finally garmenting. Characterized by an 'Inverted Duty Structure' where key inputs (e.g., dyes at 18%) are taxed higher than the output (e.g., fabric at 5%).

Applicable HSN Chapters:
Chapters 50-63 (Textiles), Chapter 41-42 (Hides, Leather)

Goods Directory & Value Chains

🌾 Wheat to Biscuit Value Chain (Agricultural Archetype)

Stage 1: Farmer → Mandi/APMC

Product: Wheat (HSN: 1001) - 0% GST (Exempt)

Hidden Charges: Mandi Cess (1-3%), Palledari, Quality Deductions

Note: Initial transaction is exempt, breaking the credit chain from start.

Stage 2: Commission Agent Processing

Services: Arhatiya Commission (2-6%), Weighing Charges, Dharmada

Note: Commission agent services subject to GST under RCM.

Stage 3: Miller → Biscuit Factory

Input: Wheat (0% GST) → Output: Wheat Flour (HSN: 1101) - 5% GST

Note: First stage where GST is levied on the good itself.

Stage 4: Biscuit Manufacturing

Inputs: Flour (5%), Sugar (5%), Fats & Oils (12%), Chocolate (18%), Packaging (18%)

Note: Represents 'Inverted Duty Structure' - higher tax on inputs than output.

Stage 5: Distribution & Retail

Output: Biscuits (HSN: 1905) - 18% GST

Note: Final product taxed at standard 18% rate with full ITC available.

⛏️ Iron Ore to TMT Bars Value Chain (Industrial Archetype)

Stage 1: Mining

Output: Iron Ore (HSN: 2601) - 5% GST

Hidden Charges: Royalty to Government, DMF levy

Note: GST applicable on royalty payments under RCM.

Stage 2: Steel Manufacturing

Inputs: Iron Ore (5%), Coking Coal (5%), Limestone (5%)

Output: TMT Steel Bars (HSN: 7214) - 18% GST

Note: Major value addition with full ITC on inputs available.

🚗 Components to Passenger Car Value Chain (Assembly Archetype)

Stage 1: Component Manufacturing

Inputs: Steel (18%), Plastics (18%)

Output: Auto Components (HSN: 8708) - 28% GST

Stage 2: Vehicle Assembly

Inputs: Various Components (28%), Tyres (28%)

Output: Passenger Car (HSN: 8703) - 28% GST + Compensation Cess (1-22%)

Note: High taxation item with both GST and significant Cess component.

🧵 Cotton to T-Shirt Value Chain (Textile Archetype)

Stage 1: Ginning & Spinning

Input: Raw Cotton (HSN: 5201) - 5% GST (RCM applicable)

Output: Cotton Yarn (HSN: 5205) - 5% GST

Note: Chain starts with 5% GST rate.

Stage 2: Weaving & Processing

Inputs: Cotton Yarn (5%), Dyes & Chemicals (18%)

Output: Finished Fabric (HSN: 5208) - 5% GST

Note: Classic Inverted Duty Structure - inputs at 18%, output at 5%.

Stage 3: Garmenting & Sale

Input: Finished Fabric (5%)

Output: T-Shirt (HSN: 6109) - 5% (≤₹1000) or 12% (>₹1000)

Note: Final GST rate depends on sale value.

GST Concepts & Compliance

Special Provisions

Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)

The recipient of the supply is liable to pay GST instead of the supplier.

Applies to Goods: Cashew nuts, Bidi wrapper leaves, Tobacco leaves, Silk yarn, Raw cotton, Lottery

Applies to Services: GTA services, Legal services by advocates, Sponsorship services, Import of services

Zero-Rated Supplies

Supplies that attract a 0% GST rate, but the supplier can claim Input Tax Credit (ITC).

Applies to: Exports of goods or services, Supplies to SEZ developer or unit

Composition Scheme

A simplified GST scheme for smaller taxpayers with aggregate turnover up to ₹1.5 crore.

Conditions: Cannot make inter-state supplies, cannot supply through e-commerce, pays tax at fixed rate, no ITC can be claimed.

Compliance Calendar

GSTR-1 - Statement of Outward Supplies

Due Date: 11th of next month (monthly filers) or 13th of month succeeding quarter (QRMP scheme)

GSTR-3B - Summary Return

Due Date: 20th of next month

Summary return of outward and inward supplies and payment of tax.

GSTR-9 - Annual Return

Due Date: 31st December of next financial year

Recent Updates

Online Gaming Taxation

Online gaming, horse racing, and casinos are taxed at 28% on the full face value of the bet or deposit.

E-invoicing Threshold

Mandatory e-invoicing for all registered persons whose aggregate turnover exceeds ₹5 crore in any preceding financial year.
